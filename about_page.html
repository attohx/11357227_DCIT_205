About

The University of Ghana is the oldest and largest public research university in Ghana, founded in 1948 as the University College of the Gold Coast. It is located in Legon, a suburb of the capital city Accra, and has over 40,000 students enrolled in various programmes at the undergraduate, postgraduate, and distance levels. The university has a vision of becoming a world-class research-intensive institution that addresses national and global challenges through teaching, learning, research, and service¹. The University of Ghana offers a wide range of academic disciplines and fields of study, organized into four colleges, six schools, 13 institutes, and 93 departments and centres. Some of the notable faculties include the School of Medicine and Dentistry, the School of Law, the School of Engineering Sciences, the School of Public Health, the School of Languages, and the School of Performing Arts. The university also has several research institutes and centres that focus on areas such as climate change, migration, development, governance, and African studies². The University of Ghana has a vibrant and diverse campus life that provides students with various opportunities to learn, grow, and have fun. Students can participate in various clubs and societies, sports and recreational activities, cultural and social events, and student leadership and governance. The university also has a strong international presence, with over 1,500 students from over 60 countries, and partnerships with several universities and organizations around the world. The university also offers financial aid and scholarships to qualified students who need assistance². The University of Ghana is a prestigious and reputable institution that strives for excellence and innovation in teaching, research, and service. It is a place where students can pursue their academic and career aspirations, and contribute to the development of Ghana and the world.
